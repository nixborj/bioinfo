# Our Work

The bioinformatics group at the International Rice Research Institute develops computational tools, analytical pipelines, and data resources to support rice genomics, breeding, and research worldwide.

Our work focuses on large-scale genomic data analysis, development of bioinformatics platforms, and training researchers in modern computational biology methods.

---

## Analyses

We support a wide range of genomic and bioinformatics analyses including:

* **Variant calling**
* **Genomics and genome comparison**
* **Allele mining**
* **High-throughput phenotyping analysis**
* **Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) and post-GWAS analyses**
* **QTL mapping**
* **Transcriptomics**

These analyses support research in rice genetics, breeding, and trait discovery.

## Major Outputs

Our work has produced several major research outputs and community resources.

### Publications

Examples include:

* **3K Rice Genome**
* **Data notes**

### Databases and Tools

We also develop and maintain bioinformatics tools and data portals used by researchers worldwide.

* **SNP-Seek**
* **SNP-Seek II**
* **Rice Galaxy**
* **RicePilaf**

These platforms provide access to rice genomic variation data, analysis pipelines, and computational resources for genomics research.

---

## Collaborative Work

Our team collaborates with scientists and institutions worldwide on bioinformatics and genomics research projects. These collaborations contribute to advancing rice science and improving crop breeding strategies.

Selected outputs from these collaborations include joint publications and shared data resources.

---

## Training and Capacity Building

We conduct regular **bioinformatics training programs** for researchers at IRRI and from institutions around the world. These trainings focus on topics such as:

* Next-generation sequencing (NGS) data analysis
* GWAS and genomic analysis
* Bioinformatics tools and workflows

Since **2022**, we have also conducted focused training activities as part of the **KOICA–UPLB–IRRI capacity-building collaboration**.

For upcoming courses and training opportunities, please visit the training pages on this site.
